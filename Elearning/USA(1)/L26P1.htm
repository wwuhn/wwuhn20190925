English USA Lesson 26, Part 1

1.

复习所学的表达。在上交了驾驶证和汽车申请表后，马丁为新车买了保险。听听马丁如何回答职员的提问。

  L26P1_1.MP3[631KB]

This is English USA, on the Voice of America. Now, Lesson 26, Part 1.

CLERK:

Good morning. May I help you?

MARTIN:

Good morning. I want to apply for a new driver's license.

CLERK:

Do you have a driver's license? 

MARTIN:

No, I don't. I lost it.

CLERK:

You don't want a new license. You want a replacement.

MARTIN:

Pardon me.

CLERK:

You want a replacement.

MARTIN:

What's that?

CLERK:

You had a license. You don't need a new license. You need a replacement.

MARTIN:

OK. I need a replacement.

CLERK:

Fine. What's your name?

MARTIN:

Martin Learner.

CLERK:

I can't find your name. Spell it for me.

MARTIN:

M-A-R-T-I-N L-E-A-R-N-E-R

CLERK:

Here it is. Where do you live?

MARTIN:

I live at fourteen-twenty-seven Grant Street, Baltimore, Maryland.

CLERK:

Good. Give me your phone number?

MARTIN:

Four-one-oh, five-five-five, six-seven-two-nine.

CLERK:

Is that a new number?

MARTIN:

No, it's an old number.

CLERK:

Thank you. Just a minute, please.

CLERK:

Go to window number four. You need to have a picture.

MARTIN:

Thank you.

CLERK:

You're welcome.

PHOTOG:

Next!

MARTIN:

I'm next.

PHOTOG:

May I have your license?

MARTIN:

Here it is.

PHOTOG:

Thanks. Stand over there. That's it. Hold it. OK. That's it.

MARTIN:

That's it?

PHOTOG:

Wait over there. I'm going to call you.

PHOTOG:

Martin Learner!

MARTIN:

I'm Martin Learner.

PHOTOG:

Here it is.

MARTIN:

Thank you.

PHOTOG:

Go to window five. You need to pay.

MARTIN:

OK. Thank you.

Can I get a new vehicle license here?

2ND CLERK:

Car or truck?

MARTIN:

Excuse me.

2ND CLERK:

Is it for a car?

MARTIN:

Yes, it is.

2ND CLERK:

What's your name?

MARTIN:

Martin Learner.

2ND CLERK:

Pardon me.

MARTIN:

I'm going to write it for you.

2ND CLERK:

OK. Oh, I see.

2ND CLERK:

Is it a new car?

MARTIN:

Yes, it is.

2ND CLERK:

Do you have a second car?

MARTIN:

No, I don't.

2ND CLERK:

Did you have an old car?

MARTIN:

Yes, I did. I sold it.

2ND CLERK:

Where do you live?

MARTIN:

Fourteen-twenty-seven, Grant Street.

2ND CLERK:

In Baltimore?

MARTIN:

Yes.

2ND CLERK:

Give me your phone number, please.

MARTIN:

Four-one-oh, five-five-five, six-seven-two-nine.
