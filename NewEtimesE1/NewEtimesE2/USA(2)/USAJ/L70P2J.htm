English USA Translation
Lesson 70, Part 2

L70P2J.MP3[642KB]

MARTIN:

See the boy who is standing over there?

看见站在那边的男孩了吗？

MALE:

The boy who is wearing the blue shirt?

穿着蓝色衬衣的那个男孩吗？

MARTIN:

Yes. What is he eating?

是的。他在吃什么？

MALE:

That's an Indian taco.

那是印第安人的玉米面豆卷。

MARTIN:

Is that typical Indian food?

是典型的印第安人食品吗？

MALE:

We borrowed it from Mexico. I think it is powwow food. We don't eat tacos in my community.

我们从墨西哥人那里学来的。我想那是帕瓦仪式的食品。在我住的社区里，我们不吃玉米面豆卷的。

MARTIN:

Is there typical Native American food?

有典型的美国土著食品吗？

MALE:

In the old days maybe. Nowadays we eat what everyone else eats. People in the Southwest eat different foods from the ones who live in the Northeast. Could we ask my wife later? I don't know much about food.

在以前也许有的。现在别人吃啥，我们吃啥。住在西南部的人们跟住在东北部的人们吃不同的食物。待会儿问我太太好吗？我对食品了解不多。

MARTIN:

Could I talk to your wife about food? I like to know about food.

我可以跟你太太谈谈食品吗？我想对食品有所了解。

MALE:

Are you hungry?

你饿了吗？

MARTIN:

No, not now. What's he wearing around his neck?

不，现在还不饿。他脖子上围的是什么？

MALE:

Who?

谁？

MARTIN:

The one who is eating the taco. I forget what you call the thing he's wearing.

那个吃着玉米面豆卷的人。我忘了你说他脖子上戴的是什么。

MALE:

That's called a bolo. Many men in the West wear those.

那叫做波乐项圈.西部很多男子都戴这种项圈。

MARTIN:

Does it mean something? Why do they wear them?

意味着什么吗？为什么要戴？

MALE:

It's for decoration. It's like the clothing for the dance. Decoration is important for celebrations.

装饰而已。就像跳舞用的服装。庆祝活动中装饰很重要。

MARTIN:

The bolo doesn't have any other meaning?

波乐项圈没有别的意思吗？

MALE:

The bolo is very simple. It's just a black cord around the neck with a piece of jewelry that slides up and down. The piece of jewelry is usually silver with some stone. Some Indians think the stone should be turquoise.

波乐项圈非常简单。它不过是绕着脖子的一条黑带子，上面有一粒可以上下滑动的珠宝。那粒珠宝通常是银加上某种宝石。有些印第安人认为这种宝石是绿宝石。

MARTIN:

Like the blue stone he is wearing?

就像他戴的那种蓝宝石？

MALE:

Yes. Some people think turquoise has spiritual qualities. Look at the other people here. You will see a lot of turquoise jewelry.

是的。有些人认为绿宝石有神灵特性。看看这里的其他人。你会看见很多绿宝石。

MARTIN:

What is that over there? Can we walk over there?

那边的是什么？我们可以走过去吗？

MALE:

That's the trading area. Do you like arts and crafts?

那是贸易区。你喜欢艺术品和工艺品吗？

MARTIN:

I like to look at them.

我喜欢看看。

MALE:

Many people come to powwows to trade or shop. Some Native Americans are doing arts and crafts that were given up or forgotten. Look at these.

许多人来帕瓦仪式交换物品或者买东西。有些土著美国人做些被放弃或忘记了的艺术品和工艺品。看看这些。

MARTIN:

What are those?

那些是什么？

MALE:

What do you think?

你说呢？

MARTIN:

Maybe something used for cooking.

可能是用来烹调的什么东西吧。

MALE:

That's right. They were simple cooking utensils made from wood.

没错。是用木头制成的简易厨具。

MARTIN:

What's that one for?

那个厨具用来干什么的？

MALE:

That's for holding a pot over the fire.

那是用来把锅悬挂在火上用的。

MARTIN:

What's this one?

这个呢？

MALE:

It's a stirring stick, probably for porridge. That one is a kind of fork for holding or turning meat over the fire. Let me show you some of the clothing.

这是一根搅拌棍，可能用来搅动粥的。那个是一种叉子，用来撑起或者翻转被烧烤的肉。我给你看一些服装吧。

MARTIN:

This is wonderful. What is it?

太棒了。这是什么？

MALE:

I don't know. Let's ask.

我不知道。找人问问。

MARTIN:

This is a wonderful dance.

这场舞蹈真棒。

MALE:

What would you like to see now?

现在你想看什么？

MARTIN:

I would like to look at that. What is it?

我想看看那个。那是什么？

MALE:

You always look for the food, don't you? I think it's time for lunch.

你总是找食品问问题，是吗？我想到吃午饭的时候了。

MARTIN:

What are they doing?

他们在干什么？

MALE:

They are making corn meal. Let's watch.

他们在煮玉米饭。我们看看。

MARTIN:

What's that they are making?

他们在做什么？
