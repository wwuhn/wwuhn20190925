English USA Translation
Lesson 64, Part 2

L64P2J.MP3[674KB]

MARTIN:

I'm at the Indianapolis 500 racetrack. Tell me who you are.

我现在位于印第安纳波利斯500赛车场的跑道上。请问你是哪位。

FORD:

Ford Johnson.

我是福特・约翰逊。

MARTIN:

Ford!?

福特！？

FORD:

I had to work with cars, didn't I?

我原来是修汽车的，是吧？

MARTIN:

Do you work on Ford cars?

你修福特汽车吗？

FORD:

No, not now.

现在不了。

MARTIN:

You're a member of Rick Sanderson's team. How many people work on the pit crew?

你是李克・桑德逊团队的成员。有多少人在维护组工作？

FORD:

During the race?

在比赛的时候吗？

MARTIN:

Yes.

是的。

FORD:

There are only five workers. Pit crews can only be five people.

只有五个工人。维护组只能有五个人。

MARTIN:

But I see more men wearing the team uniforms.

但是我看见穿着团队制服的不止五个人。

FORD:

There are rules and regulations for the race. Only five men can be in the pit when the car comes in.

赛车比赛有很多规章制度。当赛车开进来的时候，维护区里只准有五个人。

MARTIN:

Where are the other workers?

其他工人呢？

FORD:

Behind the wall.

在围墙后面。

MARTIN:

What do you do?

你们干什么？

FORD:

My co-workers change the tires. I add fuel.

我的同事们换轮胎。我加油。

MARTIN:

How long does that take?

要多长时间？

FORD:

The best time is about twelve and a half seconds.

最好的成绩是两秒半左右。

MARTIN:

Seconds! I can't believe it.

秒哇！我真不敢相信。

FORD:

That's right. Each man changes one tire. I add the fuel.

是秒。一人换一个轮胎。我加油。

MARTIN:

The fuel is dangerous, isn't it?

加油有危险，是吗？

FORD:

There have been accidents. Accidents on the track are very dangerous. The cars may go over two hundred miles an hour. In an accident the fuel is dangerous. But there are about three hundred firemen around the track.

曾经出过事故。在跑道上出事故是非常危险的。赛车的时速可能超过两百英里。发生事故时燃料就很危险。但是有大约三百名消防员守候在跑道周围。

MARTIN:

Is the pit area dangerous?

维护区危险吗？

FORD:

Not very dangerous. There are new safety rules and regulations.

不是很危险。现在有新的安全规章和制度了。

MARTIN:

Are you afraid?

你害怕吗？

FORD:

No, I'm very careful. The equipment is very good.

不怕。我非常小心。设备非常好。

MARTIN:

The equipment is very expensive, isn't it?

设备很昂贵，是吧？

FORD:

Yes. The pit equipment costs about three hundred thousand dollars.

是的。维护区的设备要花大约三十万美元。

MARTIN:

How much does it cost to have a car in the Indianapolis 500?

让一台车子到印第安纳布里斯500赛车场参赛，要花多少钱呢？

FORD:

At least one million. Most owners spend about two million.

至少一百万。大部分车主要开销两百万左右。

MARTIN:

That's a lot!

这可是大数目啊！

FORD:

It's OK when you win. The winning team gets more than a million dollars this year.

你赢了就没问题了。今年的获胜队得到一百多万美元。

MARTIN:

What if you don't win? Who pays for everything? It isn't good business for the owners, is it?

你没赢怎么办？谁为这一切付款？对车主们而言，这可不是好买卖，是吗？

FORD:

No, it's exciting. The sponsors provide most of the money. We can't race without sponsors.

没错。比赛很刺激。赞助商们提供了大部分的费用。没有赞助商我们就没法子比赛。

MARTIN:

Tell about sponsors. What do you mean?

说说赞助商吧。你的意思是指什么？

FORD:

Each team and driver have sponsors. I mean companies help pay the costs.

每个团队和车手都有赞助商。我是说各家公司帮助支付开销。

MARTIN:

Companies that make things for cars.

那些生产汽车用品的公司。

FORD:

Sure. Tires, oil, all kinds of things. Car companies too. It's like other sports, you know, like tennis. International companies.

当然了。车胎了，润滑油了，各种各样的东西。还有汽车生产厂家。就像其他体育运动，好比网球。还有跨国公司。

MARTIN:

Let me ask another question. Who's the boss on your team?

我想问另外一个问题。谁是你们团队的老板？

FORD:

The boss. That's a good question.

老板。这是个好问题。

MARTIN:

Is it the driver?

是车手吗？

FORD:

Sometimes. Hank is my supervisor. We usually work as a team, but Hank organizes everything. He's my boss. Hank's boss is the owner.

有时候是。汉克是我的主管。我们通常组成一个团队，不过汉克把一切组织起来。他是我的老板。汉克的老板是车主。

FORD:

Excuse me, Martin. It's time for the driver to make a pit stop.

对不起，马丁。车手要做加油维护停靠了。

MARTIN:

May I watch?

我可以在一旁观看吗？

FORD:

Sure. Stand over there beside Hank.

可以。请站到那边去，站在汉克旁边。
