Hi. This is Ray McDonald and Nancy Beardsley with words and phrases from the English USA worksheet for Lesson 52. We will discuss how the parts of speech are used together:

[Basic Sentence Structures] [基本句型]

Verb + noun  {动词 + 名词}
This sentence is made from a verb plus a noun:

Open the door.

开门。

Verb + adverb {动词 + 副词}
This one has a verb plus an adverb:
Turn right. 向右转。

Noun (or pronoun) + "be"-verb + noun (or pronoun) {名词（或代词） + 系动词“be” + 名词（或代词）}
These sentences are made with a noun or pronoun plus a "be"-verb plus another noun or pronoun:
Martin Learner is a reporter. 马丁・雷纳是一位记者。
He is a father. 他当了父亲

Noun (or pronoun) + "be"-verb + adjective  {名词（或代词） + 系动词“be” + 形容词}
A noun or pronoun plus a "be"-verb plus an adjective make sentence like these:
The children are intelligent. 孩子们很聪明。
They are busy. 他们很忙。

Noun (or pronoun) + "be"-verb + adverb    {名词（或代词） + 系动词“be” + 副词}
A noun or pronoun plus a "be"-verb plus an adverb produces this sentence:
It is there. 东西在那儿。

Noun (or pronoun) + verb    {名词（或代词） + 动词}
These sentences are built from a noun or pronoun plus a verb:
Parents listen. 父母在听。
They learn. 他们在学。

Noun (or pronoun) + verb + noun (or pronoun)    {名词（或代词） + 动词 + 名词}
A noun or pronoun plus a verb plus another noun or pronoun makes sentences like these:
The woman drives a car. 那位妇女开车。
We read the book. 我们读这本书。

Noun (or pronoun) + verb + adverb    {名词（或代词） + 动词 + 副词}
Using a noun or pronoun plus a verb plus an adverb gives you this sentence:
You live there. 你住在那里。

Noun + "be"-verb + verb + (noun, pronoun, adverb)    {名词 + 系动词“be” + 动词 + （名词，代词，副词）}
A noun plus a "be"-verb plus a verb plus a noun, pronoun or adverb produces these sentences:
The boy is buying a shirt. 那男孩正在买一件衬衣。
People were visiting them. 人们访问他们。
You are going downstairs. 你正在下楼。

Question word + "be"-verb + noun (or pronoun)    {疑问词 + 系动词“be” + 名词（或代词）}
A question word plus a "be"-verb plus a noun or pronoun makes sentences like these:

Who are you? 你是谁？
What was that? 那是什么？
Where is Eileen? 爱莲在哪里？
How were they? 他们怎么样？
When was it? 那件事（发生）在什么时候？

Question verb + noun (or pronoun) + verb + (noun, adverb)    {疑问词[助动词] + 名词（或代词）+ 动词 +（名词、副词）}
A question verb plus a noun or pronoun plus a verb plus a noun or adverb raises different questions:
Does Mr. Learner drive a car? 雷纳先生开车吗？
Do they live here? 他们住在这里吗？
Did the teachers study the animals? 那些老师研究动物吗？
