Everything But The Kitchen Sink (almost everything you could possibly think to include.. )

To bring a great many things with you when you go someplace is to bring "everything but the kitchen sink." This is to say that you really brought a lot of stuff! You included almost everything you could possibly carry.

Example: "Wow-your suitcase is huge! What do you have in there? Everything but the kitchen sink?" When we decide what to include we want to bring "everything" useful "but" some things like "the kitchen sink" are not possible to include; so we bring everything "but" the kitchen sink.

Example: "Whenever we go camping my wife wants to bring everything but the kitchen sink!" "Everything but the kitchen sink" means almost everything you could possibly think to include. Example: "My dad's new luxury car is great; it has everything but the kitchen sink!"

Fair And Fair Alike ( the same rules should apply evenly to all ... )

"Fair and fair alike" reminds us that what is fair for one person should be fair for another; the rules of fairness must apply evenly to all.

Example: "Peter is getting two candies but I am only getting one!" Answer: "You had two candies earlier today, so now you are even; fair and fair alike." Children often say fair and fair alike because they are very concerned about what is and is not fair.

Example: "Michael stayed home to take care of your sister last night, so tonight it is your turn. Fair and fair alike." "Fair and fair alike" means that the same ("alike") rules must be applied evenly ("alike") for it to be "fair".

Finding Your Feet ( feeling more familiar in what you are doing ... )

To "find your feet" is to become comfortable acting in a certain type of situation. Example: "I've only been at this job for a few months: I'm just finding my feet." You have gained the needed skills and perform the job well, once you have found your feet.

Example: "Don't worry about it. We will help you while you are finding your feet."

Fixed In Your Ways ( not wanting to change how you do things ... )

When you have already decided how you will do things, and you do not want to change, you are "fixed in your ways." Example: "I tried many different things when I was young, but I'm older now and more fixed in my ways." People usually become fixed in their ways because they are comfortable and happy doing things one way, or because they are just lazy and unwilling to change. Example: "Sometimes it is hard to accept that your parents are fixed in their ways."

Flash In The Pan ( a sudden success that disappears quickly ... )

A "flash in the pan" is success which begins suddenly and ends so quickly that it is like a "flash" of fire "in the" frying "pan".

Example: "That was a great paper you wrote. Was it just a flash in the pan? or are you going to be able to do it again and again?" A person who is a "flash in the pan" rises to success suddenly and disappears just as quickly.

Example: "What a great first year he had, but after that... nothing!" Answer: "Just another flash in the pan."

From Rags To Riches ( from poor to wealthy ... )

A person who was poor and becomes rich goes "from rags to riches". The "rags" represent the poor old clothing that they wore before they came into their "riches". Quote from a famous song: "You know I'll never go from rags to riches!" Example: "My uncle is a real rags to riches story." He was very poor but now he is rich.

Get Up On The Wrong Side Of The Bed ( in a bad mood because you are having a bad day ... )

When it seems like you have been having a bad day all day, one might say that you "got up on the wrong side of the bed."

Example: "What's wrong with her today?" Answer: "It looks like she got up on the wrong side of the bed." Sometimes when we get a bad start to our day, it continues throughout the day and people notice; it is as if we started our day ("got up") in the wrong way ("on the wrong side of the bed") which has affected everything else that happened since.

Example: "I told you to pick up your things! And don't play your music so loudly!" Answer: "Wow, looks like someone got up on the wrong side of the bed!"

Example: "Don't start yelling at me just because you got up on the wrong side of the bed."

 Give Him The Slip ( to escape from that person ... )

To "give someone the slip" is to escape, or get away from that person.

Example: "Quick- there is a police car behind us; let's give him the slip!" Something "slips" (verb) when it gets away from where it is supposed to be. A "slip" (noun) is an old English word for a fake coin; if you bought something and paid for it with a fake coin, you gave the seller "the slip". To do something to get away from someone is to "give him the slip."

Example: "My brother will be at the movie tonight. Afterwards, let's give him the slip and go to a party." The moment you got away from that person is the moment you gave him the slip.

Go For Broke ( bet everything on the chance to win big ... )

You are "broke" when you have no money; to "go for broke" is to risk losing everything for one chance to win big.

Example: "Are you sure you want to bet all of your money on that one horse?" Answer: "I'm going for broke!" "Go for broke" also means to take a risk, try your best, and give all your energy to something.

Example: "Do you think that girl will dance with me if I ask her to?" Answer: "Sure: go for broke!"

Example: "The way to be successful is to decide exactly what you want, then go for broke."

 Great Minds Think Alike ( people with great minds think like each other ... )

When you and another person think the same thing at the same time, you might say "great minds think alike."

Example: "I was just thinking to call you, then the phone rang and it was you calling me!" Answer: "Great minds think alike!" It is fun to say "great minds think alike" because you are making a connection with the other person by saying that the two of you, in thinking the same thing at the same time, both have great minds.

Example: "I have decided that this summer I am going to learn how to scuba dive." Answer: "Me too! I have already paid for the course. Great minds think alike!"
