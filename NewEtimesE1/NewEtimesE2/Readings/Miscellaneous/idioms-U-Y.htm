Under The Gun ( working under pressure ... )

When you are under the gun you are trying to achieve something under tremendous pressure. "Everyone at the office has been working under the gun since the new manager arrived." There may be a threat you face, such as a [time] deadline or a [sales] quota you need to meet. You are under the gun when you are working hard to meet certain specific deadlines. "We won't be able to leave until eight today; we're under the gun to complete this issue."

Under The Weather ( not feeling well ... )

To say that someone is "under the weather" is to say that they are not feeling very well.

Example: "What's wrong?" Answer: "I'm a bit under the weather." They probably have a simple cold or flu which will go away quickly.

Example: "It's nothing serious; I'm just a bit under the weather." Being "under the weather" reminds us that a quick change in the weather can affect our health and the way we feel.

Up For Grabs ( available for anyone to take ... )

Something which is free and available to anyone is "up for grabs."

Example: "Does anyone want my ice cream? It's up for grabs." If something is up for grabs, and you want it, you had better grab it quickly before someone else does.

Example: "I have decided that I am finally moving out of this city!" Answer: "Does that mean your apartment is up for grabs?" Another example: "Quick- that table is up for grabs; let's get it before someone else does."

Variety Is The Spice Of Life ( doing different things keeps life exciting...)

" Variety is the spice of life" expresses the general idea that it is good to try and do different things. Just as a cook uses different spices to create the flavours in a great meal, a person who enjoys interesting experiences will have a great life. Example: " We were originally planning to go to Mexico on our vacation this year - like we did last year - but we decided to go to Egypt in stead. Variety is the spice of life!"

Water Under The Bridge ( something we have forgotten about ... )

We say "water under the bridge" to refer to past experiences and conflicts which we have decided to forget. This idiom compares these memories of the past to water which has passing under [the] bridge. Example: "Aren't you still angry about what he said?" Answer: "No, that was a long time ago. It's all water under the bridge."

Wear Your Heart On Your Sleeve ( you express your emotions freely, for all to see ... )

People who "wear their heart on their sleeve" express their emotions openly and freely; you can see how they feel as easily as if they were "wearing" their "heart" on the "sleeve" of their shirt.

Example: "She's this kind of player who never hides how she's feeling; she always wears her heart on her sleeve; you'll don't even need to look at the score to know if she's winning or losing!" People who wear their heart on their sleeve do not hold back their emotions, for good or for bad; it is always clear how they feel in each moment.

Example: "Some people love him and some people hate him; but my brother always lets you know how he feels; he wears his heart on his sleeve."

 What They Don't Know Won't Hurt Them ( they don't need to know ... )

When there is no need to tell someone something, you can say "what they don't know won't hurt them."

Example: "Don't tell your father what happened; what he doesn't know won't hurt him." Since he won't be hurt by not knowing, there is no need to tell him.

 When In Rome, Do As The Romans Do ( when you visit a new place you should try new experiences and to do as the people do who are from that place ... )

"When in Rome, do as the Romans do" says that when you are in a different place ("when in Rome") it is important to try new experiences and do as the people ("the Romans") do who are from that place.

Example: "I can't eat that; it looks horrible!" Answer: "Give it a try! When in Rome, do as the Romans do." People from different places have different ways of acting; you should not go to a new place and act exactly as you would at home. "When in Rome, do as the Romans do" says that you should try to do as people do who are from the place you are visiting.

Example: "Are you sure we should eat this with our hands?" Answer: "Why not? All of these people are eating it that way. When in Rome, do as the Romans do!" The city of Rome was the capitol of the great Roman empire; there were many strange and interesting things to do when people visited Rome. "When in Rome, do as the Romans do" says you should not be uncomfortable when you are in a new place and see people ("Romans") doing new things; give it a try and do as the people do who are from that place ("do as the Romans do").

Example: "Sorry, I can't sing now; back home we never sing in front of other people." Answer: "Oh, come on; give it a try! When in Rome, do as the Romans do!"

When It Rains, It Pours ( when things happen, they happen all at once and in large amounts ... )

"When it rains, it pours" describes how after there has been no rain for a long time it will suddenly rain a lot all at once.

Example: "Sometimes we have no customers for two or three hours then suddenly we get 20 people all at once; when it rains, it pours!" When there is a lot of rain all at once we say that it is "pouring". "When it rains, it pours" means that when something happens after a long pause, it happens in large amounts.

Example: "It looks like everyone in our department is sick all at the same time. What are we going to do? When it rains, it pours!"

 When Pigs Fly ( something that will never happen ... )

Pigs are animals that do not fly. "When pigs fly" is a time that will never come, because pigs will never fly. Example: "Do you think you will ever work at that company again?" Answer: "When pigs fly!" "When pigs fly" is an informal way to joke that you will never do something. Example: "Would you ever take her on a date?" Answer: "Sure- when pigs fly!" Pigs are heavy animals that stay close to the ground; "when pigs fly" is a time that will never come.

 Wine And Dine ( to treat someone to expensive food and drink ... )

You "wine and dine" someone when you take them out to nice restaurants and treat them to expensive food and drink.

Example: "How was your business trip?" Answer: "Great- they wined and dined us all week." You wine and dine someone in order to build a strong personal relationship with them. Example: "That man is really is really crazy about my sister. He has been wining and dining her all month."

 With Your Back Up Against The Wall ( restrained from acting as you would like ... )

When we are in a defensive position and have to make choices that we would not like to make, we say that we have our "back up against the wall". The wall represents an external force that causes us to act in a way that we would not like. This force may also prevent us from acting when we would like to. "I'm sorry I can't help you; I've got my back up against the wall."

Word Of Mouth ( something you know because you heard it from other people ... )

Something that you know because you heard it from others, not because you saw it or read about it, is "word of mouth".

Example: "Did you hear they finally arrested him?" Answer: "That's not true; it's just word of mouth." Reply: "No, it is true; I saw it on TV!" When you only hear something word of mouth you cannot be absolutely certain that it is true because you don't know for sure from whose mouth the word started.

 Example: "I heard at school that they are going to hold the concert in the downtown area." Answer: "That is just word of mouth; there is no way to know if it is true yet." We do rely on what other people tell us, so word of mouth is one of the ways that we get our information.

Example: "Where did you hear about that?" Answer: "Just word of mouth."

Your Guess Is As Good As Mine ( I don't know ... )

When you respond to someone's question by saying "your guess is an good as mine" you are letting them know that you can not speak with certainty about something. If you were to answer their question, you would only be guessing.

Example: "Excuse me, what time does the bus arrive?" Answer: "Your guess is as good as mine; I almost never take the bus." Neither you nor they have enough information to answer with certainty. Your next step, if it were important, would probably be to get more information so you could speak with more clarity.
