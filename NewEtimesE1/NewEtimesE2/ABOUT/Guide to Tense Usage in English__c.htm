 Present simple tense（一般现在时）

用于描述确定的事实和一般的规律。

'A banana is never quite straight.' 
'Malaysia exports rubber.'

也用于习惯性的动作和行为。

The President gets up at five and starts work at seven.

通常用于这几个感知性动词：sound, seem, appear, smell, taste, look and feel (注意： look 和 feel 也可用于进行时) Going to Fiji sounds just great because the beaches appear less crowded and the prices seem reasonable. 
This French bread smells quite fresh, tastes delicious, feels very soft and looks just great.
COMPARE: 'I am not feeling very well today' and 'You are looking wonderful in that new dress, my dear, but where are the curtains?'

用于 hear, see (例外的情况是see的意思为 'to meet')

'I hear footsteps. Quick, someone's coming!'
'I see you don't understand what I mean.'
COMPARE: 'She is seeing the dentist tomorrow.'

始终用于所谓的陈述性动词(state verbs)，例如 agree, approve of, believe, belong to, consider (hold an opinion), consist of, contain, cost, depend, disagree, gather (understand), hate, have (own), know, like, loathe, love, mean, own, need, possess, prefer, realize, regret, remember, resemble, suppose, think (hold an opinion), understand, want, wish, 等等。

 'Some people believe in UFOs, but I think they're misguided.'

'Henry regrets what he did and wishes to make amends.'

'Although, of course, I don't normally approve of gossip, I do like her new autobiography. It contains a number of sensational revelations about the world of showbiz!'

通常用于所谓述行性动词 (即所说的话实际上构成了一个动作），例如 accept, acknowledge, admit, advise, apologize, assume, deny, guarantee, hope, inform, predict, promise, recommend, suggest, warn, 等等。

 'He admits he made a big mistake, acknowledges full responsibility, accepts the consequences, apologizes from the bottom of his heart and promises not to do it again.'

用于由别人制定好的计划。 

 'His ship sails at dawn.'
'The next train leaves at half-past six.'

通常用于讲笑话或滑稽的故事。

 'A man wanders into a restaurant and says he can eat a horse. The waiter tells him he's come to the wrong place.'

体育比赛直播解说员喜欢用，以求用词少有能传达一种激动人心和开门见山的感觉。

'Agassi leads four games to one in the first set.' 
'The crowd roars as Tyson takes a huge bite out of Holyfield's ear.'

常见于报纸标题，以求精简，因为篇幅昂贵。

 'Iraq Invades Kuwait'
'Man Steals Clock, Faces Time'
'Fake Cardiologist Breaks Woman's Heart'

 Present continuous tense（现在进行时）

用于说话时正在进行的行为动作或事件。

'The electrician is mending a fuse.'
'It's snowing.'
'In London John is staying at the Savoy.'

用于自己制定的计划，通常多是近期的计划。

 'Lucia's leaving for Milan after lunch.'
'Dan and Crystal are getting married in June.'

也用于计划要长期做的事情。

 'He's studying hard to become a doctor.'
'Joe Liebermann's running for President.' 

用于高频率副词，表示不赞成某种惹人生气的不良习惯。 

 'He is always complaining.'
'She's forever losing her keys.'

用于为用现在一般时（the present simple）讲述的笑话或滑稽的故事设置场景。

 'This guy is sitting all by himself in a bar looking pretty inebriated, so the barman refuses to serve him another drink.'

to be 的正在进行时用于觉得某人行为一时反常时的表述。

 'He really is being stupid' (meaning this person is normally more sensible).
COMPARE: 'He really is stupid' (meaning he is stupid the whole time).

必须用于have 做行为动词（active verb）时 。

 'She is having another baby / filet steak for dinner / a shower / a heart attack / etc.' 

记住所谓的陈述性动词（state verbs） 不能用于正在进行时。

 'She is having has a lot of money.'
'She is knowing knows how to fly a plane'
'He is preferring prefers coffee to tea.'

 Present perfect tense（现在完成时）

用于强调过去开始最近完成的活动的当前结果。 'Someone has eaten my sandwiches' (which explains why the plate is empty and I'll have to go hungry).
用于强调最近发生的事件的当前的结果。  'I've lost my passport' (hence I can't leave the country).
美国英语一般过去时表达个人的看法。 这种用法不像英国英语那样精妙，能清楚地把最近发生的事件与比较久远的事件区分开来。 'I lost my passport.' (Today? Last week? Last year?)
用于报刊标题或者想强调发生了某事而不是强调具体何时发生了某事。 'Two lions have escaped from Chessington Zoo.'
'Powerful tornadoes have hit Mississippi, Louisiana and Arkansas'
用于already, just, yet。 'Samantha has already left, but Cindy has just arrived, so I guess the party hasn't finished yet.'
注意美国英语通常将 already, just and yet用于一般过去时。  'Samantha already left, but Cindy just arrived, so I guess the party didn't finish yet.' 
用于指一个人出生以来的全部人生经历。 'Dan has traveled a great deal, but he has never been to Greenland.'
用于有ever 的问句，询问是否有生以来经历过某种事情。 'Have you ever seen a straight banana?'
'Has Chuck ever done an honest day's work in his life?'
美国英语则通常将ever跟一般过去时连用。 'Did you ever see a straight banana?' 
'Did Chuck ever do an honest day's work in his life?'
现在完成时用于量化说明某件完成的事情或者目前取得的进展。 'Harry has driven 200 miles since breakfast.'
"Meg has saved $8,000 toward her new BMW.'

 Present perfect continuous tense (现在完成进行时)

用于强调在说话之前正处于进行状态的动作行为，该动作行为对目前的局面（状况）有影响。 'She has been using a computer all day' 
(so her eyes are now bloodshot). 
'Someone has been eating my sandwiches' (so half of them are missing).
用于跟 for 或 since 连用，以表达某进行着的或持续的动作行为进行了多长时间。 'Harry has been driving for three hours.'
'Meg has been saving for her BMW for ten months.'
在很多情况下现在完成进行时可以跟一般现在完成时(present perfect simple)替换使用，尽管使用后者时给人的感觉是变化可能即将来临。 'I've been living here for ten years (in other words, I feel almost like a native).'
'I have lived here for ten years (so perhaps it's about time I moved on to pastures greener).'
注意所谓的陈述动词(state verbs) 不能用于现在进行时。 'I have been knowing known Samantha for 19 years'.

 Past simple tense （一般过去时）

用于对在过去某一特定时间完成的活动或事件的描述，该活动或事件或者是已知的，或者是由一个时间表达语所说明的。 Manchester United thrashed Chelsea 4:1.
The ice sculptures attracted many visitors.
Many of the bars closed at midnight.
用于两个或更多的在过去按先后次序而不是平行地同时发生的活动。 I went into town at ten, booked my summer holiday at the travel agent's, ate lunch at Pizza Hut, saw the new Bond film at the Odeon cinema, did my shopping for the weekend and arrived home in time for tea at four.
与绘画中的前景的作用相当。用于故事中的某个动作行为（过去进行时用于布景)。 The rock group were performing when the earthquake struck. Nobody noticed.
用于频率性副词结合，谈论过去时间内重复性的动作或事件，would 以及 used 也会被用来谈论过去的习惯和常规或例行性事务。 Former British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher often only slept four hours a night. She would go to bed at one in the morning and get up at five to read the morning papers. The first thing she used to check was what they were saying about her.
用于表示感知的动词(verbs of perception): sound, seem, appear, smell, taste, look 以及 feel (注意 look 和 feel 也可以用于进行时）。 The bread smelt fresh, tasted delicious, felt very soft and looked just great.
COMPARE: 'Max wasn't feeling very well today. He really was looking under the weather.'
始终用于所谓的陈述性动词，例如 agree, approve of, believe, belong to, consider (hold an opinion), consist, contain, cost, depend, disagree, gather (understand), hate, have (own), know, like, loathe, love, mean, own, need, possess, prefer, realize, regret, remember, resemble, suppose, think (hold an opinion), understand, want, wish, etc. 'The minister was agreeing agreed to resign even though he wasn't thinking didn't think he was needing needed to.'
通常用于所谓的述行性动词（performative verbs，即所说的话实际上构成了一个动作)，例如 accept, acknowledge, admit, advise, apologize, assume, bet, deny, guarantee, hope, inform, predict, promise, recommend, suggest, warn, etc.

'Although the rich oil sheik promised (was promising) the Hollywood actress a million dollars in cash, a new Mercedes and a house in Palm Beach, her lawyer advised her not to marry him.'

 Past continuous tense （过去进行时）

过去进行时与绘画中的背景相对应。 用于为一般过去时所报道的行为动作设置场景。 'I was sitting in my armchair looking up at the night sky. The moon was beaming brightly. All the stars were twinkling. Then it came to me: I had to get the roof fixed!'
用于描述在过去某一特定时刻当某一更为短暂的事件发生的时候正在进行的临时性动作或事件。 'While I was waiting for the ferry I ate lunch in a sushi bar." 
'While I was wolfing down my sushi a small piece of fish started moving.'
用于平行进行的两个持续时间相当的动作。 'I was washing the car while my wife was cleaning the house.'
记住所谓的陈述性动词（state verbs） 不能用于正在进行时。 'I was knowing knew Samantha very well'.

 Past perfect tense（过去完成时）

用于谈论在过去之前（pre-past）即在一般过去时（the past simple）所谈及的后来的行为或事件发生之前就已经完成了的行为或事件。 'I had just prepared a candlelight dinner for two when the Jehovah's Witnesses called.'
'Jacky, who was quite breathless, had climbed ten flights of stairs.'
'Mandy had studied Finnish for 3 years before she emigrated to Finland.'
如果第二个行为是第一个行为的直接的结果，那么，两个行为都用一般过去时描述。 'When the artist had finally appeared on stage, everyone applauded'. 
用于报道过去始终抱憾未能实现的意图。 'The boss had hoped to slip off to the golf course for the rest of the afternoon but head office wanted to speak to him about disappointing sales figures.'

 Past perfect continuous tense （过去完成进行时）

用于报道在过去一直进行或者持续到所发生的某一后来事件之前夕的某件有趣的或与之直接相关的行为。 'When the chemistry teacher returned to the lab, he sniffed and stopped smiling. Someone had been making a stink bomb.' 
'Police arrested the chief executive whose company had been cooking the books.'
记住所谓的陈述性动词（state verbs） 不能用于正在进行时。 'In 1994 I had already been knowing known Samantha for 10 years'.

 Future simple tense （一般将来时）

WILL: 于表示将来。(不含任何意志力的因素) 'The sun will rise tomorrow morning.'
WILL: 用于根据自己对别人性格的了解所作的推断。 'Linda will help you, I'm sure.'
WILL: 用于普通的非正式的请求，以及给下属下命令时。 'Darling, will you post this letter for me?'
'Sally, will you show Mr. Anderson to the accounts department, please?'
重读时的WILL: 用于强调表达对他人的坏习惯的愤怒。 'My husband will always invite his friends round for a drink just as I'm putting the kids to bed!'
WILL/SHALL: 用于自发的提议或者说话时的计划，或者对某事表示同意。 'If you do decide to buy this car model, sir, we'll include a satellite navigation system.' 
'Okay, I'll ask my bank manager for a loan.'
WILL/SHALL: 用于许诺。 'Don't worry, I won't/shan't tell a soul!'
SHALL: 有时用WILL于第一人称和第三人称单数和复数中，用更正规的风格表示将来，特别是在涉及到意志力的时候。 'I shall (will) be late this evening.'
'We shan't (won't) go that nightclub anymore; the prices are exorbitant.'
'We shall overcome!'
'I shall succeed!'
SHALL: 用于寻求别人对提议或建议的赞同或支持。 'Shall I buy you a watch for your birthday?'
'Shall we all go out to dinner?'
SHALL: 用于探诱出更多的信息资料 'Which restaurant shall we go to?'
GOING TO: 用于谈论在谈话之前就已经做好的计划。  'I'm going to buy a new digital camera. My old one doesn't seem to produce sharp enough pictures.'
GOING TO: 用于预测可能发生或难免要发生的事情，因为所有的迹象都已经出现了。 'Look over there. That crazy driver's lost control. He's going to crash!'
'I feel awful after that raw fish. I think I'm going to throw up.'
通常把现在进行时用于自己制定的计划或依某人自由意愿行事的时间表，特别是在最近的将来计划或时间表中，替代GOING TO。 'We're having a party on Friday night.'
'She's leaving home right after breakfast and driving all the way up to Tallahassee in her grandma's old car.'
 通常把现在进行时用于GO和COME表示一般将来时，而不用GOING TO。 'He is going to go to New York after he leaves Washington.'
'The Smiths are going to come coming home from Canada next spring'.

用现在一般时表示由他人决定的计划。

'He flies to Cairo on business at noon tomorrow.'

 Future continuous tense （将来进行时）

用于根据预测会在将来某个特别时间发生的动作或事件。

'The kids will be sleeping when I get home.'
'Some Japanese schoolboys will no doubt still be donning 19th century black Prussian military uniforms in a hundred years' time.'

用于先前的安排或决定产生的结果而导致的将来事件。

'As you know, I'll be working overtime this evening.'
'Nancy will be staying at her parents' home over Christmas (she always does).'

可以用来替代现在进行时(present continuous) 表示将来意义。 'She'll be leaving home after breakfast.'
也用于非常客气和有礼貌地询问他人的将来计划。 'Will you be needing your laptop at work today?'
'Is IBM going to be recruiting any new personnel in the near future?' 
记住所谓的陈述性动词（state verbs） 不能用于正在进行时。 'The museum is well sign-posted, so you will be knowing know which way to go.'

 Future perfect tense （将来完成时）

用于根据预测在将来的某个特定时间将会被完成的动作或事件。 'No matter what their academic performance, many students at Japan's most prestigious universities will have found a job one whole year before they graduate.'
用于对根据预测在将来某一给定时间将会完成的进展作出量化表达。 'He smokes 20 a day, so this time next year he will have happily puffed his way through another 584 meters of cigarette.'

 Future perfect continuous tense（将来完成进行时）

用于根据预测在将来某一时刻仍在进行的动作或事件。 'By the end of 2003 we will have been flying in planes for 100 years.'
记住所谓的陈述性动词（state verbs） 不能用于正在进行时。 'Next summer I will have been knowing known Samantha for 20 years.'
