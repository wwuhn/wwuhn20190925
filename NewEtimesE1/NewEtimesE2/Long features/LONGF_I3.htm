Emily Dickinson [F022]
Emily Dickinson has become part of our language without really being part of our history. Some see her as the last poet of an early American tradition. Others see her as the first modern American poet. Each reader seems to find a different Emily Dickinson. She remains as  mysterious as she was when she was alive.

Feature 30

  Ralph Waldo Emerson [F030]
What the American Revolution did for the nation's politics, Emerson did for its culture. When he began writing and speaking in the Eighteen-Thirties, conservatives saw him as radical -- wild and dangerous. But to the young, he spoke words of self-dependence -- a new language of freedom. He was the first to bring them a truly American spirit.
