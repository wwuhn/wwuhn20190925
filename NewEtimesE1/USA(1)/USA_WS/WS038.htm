Now, some grammar notes:

　

The words we use to describe the physical features or characteristics of people are adjectives. In English, we use adjectives just before the noun they describe. This may occur in two places in a sentence, before or after the verb. First, before the subject of the sentence.

我们通常使用形容词来描述人体特征。在英语里，形容词出现在所修饰的名词前构成名词短语。名词短语在句子里出现在动词之前或之后。首先，形容词出现在句子主语之前。

　

The tall boy is my brother.

那个高个男孩是我的兄弟。

　

That short woman is talking to Martin.

那个矮个女人在跟马丁说话。

　

Her long fingers are beautiful.

她长长的手指很漂亮。

　

Second, the adjective can occur after a "be" verb.

第二，形容词可以跟在be动词后。

　

Mr. Kovisar is fat.

戈维萨很胖。

　

She is pretty.

她可爱。

　

Your hair is brown.

你的头发是棕色的。

　

Or, It can occur after verbs with nouns.

形容词或者修饰跟在动词后的名词。

　

She has long fingers.

她长着长长的手指。

　

He is the one with short hair.

他就是那个长着短头发的人。

　

Howard's sister plays an old violin.

怀沃的姐姐弹着一把旧小提琴。
